## The Trip Packing Tax

Every work trip starts the same way. I stare at a pile of chargers on my desk and try to figure out which combination of wall blocks, cables, and battery banks will keep seven devices alive for the week. iPhone, Apple Watch, MacBook, iPad, AirPods Max, AirPods Pro, and a battery bank that itself needs charging.

It's not hard, exactly. It's just _annoying_. Every device has its own charging input. Some share USB-C, one still uses Lightning (looking at you, AirPods Max), and the Apple Watch needs its magnetic puck. Then there's the optimization puzzle: which wall block powers what, what can charge through the battery bank's passthrough, what needs to swap in the morning after something else finishes overnight.

I've done this mental exercise dozens of times. I never write it down, so I re-derive it from scratch every trip. It takes 15 minutes of staring at cables, second-guessing myself, and inevitably throwing in one extra charger "just in case."

## This Time I Described the Problem Instead

I opened Claude and typed out my devices, their charging inputs, and the wall blocks I own. No spreadsheet, no diagram, just a plain description of the constraints:

```prompt title="Claude Desktop Prompt"
Here are my devices and what charges them. Here are the wall blocks I have. The Anker battery bank has built-in USB-C output cables and supports passthrough charging. Minimize what I pack.
```

That's it. Within a couple minutes Claude had mapped out the full charging topology: which block powers what, what chains through the battery bank, what swaps in the morning after the big draws finish overnight.

The answer was **2 wall blocks + 1 battery bank + 3 cables**. Six items to charge seven devices. The 65W block stays home because the 67W does everything it does and then some.
